Individualized  Sepsis  Multi-Treatment  Effect  Prediction  Using  Causal  
Transformer
 
1.  Introduction  
Sepsis  is  a  time-critical  condition  in  which  outcomes  depend  not  only  on  disease  severity,  
but
 
also
 
on
 
whether
 
the
 
right
 
treatment
 
is
 
given
 
at
 
the
 
right
 
time.
 
In
 
practice,
 
clinicians
 
adjust
 
antibiotics,
 
intravenous
 
fluids,
 
and
 
vasopressors
 
as
 
the
 
patient’s
 
condition
 
changes.
 
However,
 
most
 
deep
 
learning
 
work
 
in
 
sepsis
 
still
 
focuses
 
on
 
early
 
warning
 
or
 
mortality
 
prediction.
 
These
 
models
 
are
 
useful
 
for
 
identifying
 
risk,
 
however
 
they
 
do
 
not
 
directly
 
answer
 
the
 
treatment
 
question
 
of
 
which
 
intervention
 
is
 
most
 
likely
 
to
 
help
 
a
 
specific
 
patient
 
at
 
a
 
specific
 
time.
 
This
 
proposal
 
focuses
 
on
 
individualized
 
treatment
 
effect
 
prediction
 
in
 
sepsis.
 
The
 
aim
 
is
 
to
 
build
 
a
 
causal
 
deep
 
learning
 
model
 
that
 
estimates
 
how
 
different
 
treatment
 
choices
 
may
 
change
 
short-term
 
clinical
 
outcomes.
 
Rather
 
than
 
treating
 
sepsis
 
care
 
as
 
a
 
single
 
binary
 
treatment
 
problem,
 
the
 
proposed
 
study
 
models
 
it
 
as
 
a
 
multi-action
 
decision
 
problem
 
involving
 
antibiotics,
 
fluids,
 
and
 
vasopressors.
 
2.  Methodology  
The  data  will  be  extracted  from  MIMIC-IV  and  PhysioNet  2019  Sepsis  datasets  of  which  
are
 
split
 
into
 
training,
 
validation,
 
and
 
test
 
sets
 
using
 
a
 
70:15:15
 
ratio.
 
The
 
groupings
 
will
 
consist
 
of
 
adult
 
sepsis
 
patients
 
identified
 
based
 
on
 
the
 
Sepsis-3
 
clinical
 
criteria.
 
Patient
 
records
 
will
 
be
 
aligned
 
around
 
sepsis
 
recognition
 
and
 
converted
 
into
 
hourly
 
time-series
 
data.
 
Each
 
hourly
 
step
 
will
 
include
 
demographics,
 
comorbidities,
 
vital
 
signs,
 
laboratory
 
values,
 
organ
 
support
 
variables,
 
treatment
 
history,
 
and
 
outcome
 
labels.
 
The
 
preprocessing
 
pipeline
 
will
 
convert
 
irregular
 
ICU
 
records
 
into
 
an
 
hourly
 
format.
 
Continuous
 
variables
 
will
 
be
 
grouped
 
into
 
one-hour
 
bins,
 
extreme
 
values
 
will
 
be
 
capped,
 
and
 
features
 
will
 
be
 
normalized
 
using
 
training-set
 
statistics
 
only.
 
To
 
handle
 
missing
 
data,
 
the
 
model
 
will
 
use
 
three
 
signals
 
where
 
relevant:
 
the
 
current
 
value,
 
a
 
missing-value
 
flag,
 
and
 
the
 
time
 
since
 
the
 
last
 
measurement.
 
This
 
keeps
 
the
 
information
 
contained
 
in
 
missing-data
 
patterns,
 
which
 
is
 
common
 
in
 
ICU
 
records.
 
Treatments
 
will
 
be
 
encoded
 
as
 
time-varying
 
actions,
 
including
 
antibiotic
 
initiation
 
or
 
escalation,
 
fluid
 
bolus
 
category,
 
and
 
vasopressor
 
intensity
 
category.
 
The
 
proposed
 
model
 
is
 
a
 
causal
 
transformer
 
with
 
three
 
main
 
components.
 
First,
 
a
 
Transformer
 
encoder
 
will
 
learn
 
the
 
patient’s
 
evolving
 
condition
 
from
 
the
 
hourly
 
time-series
 
record.
 
Second,
 
a
 
treatment-probability
 
and
 
balancing
 
block
 
will
 
estimate
 
how
 
treatments
 
are
 
assigned
 
and
 
reduce
 
bias
 
from
 
non-random
 
treatment
 
selection.
 
Third,
 
an
 
outcome
 
decoder
 
will
 
simulate
 
short-term
 
outcomes
 
under
 
different
 
treatment
 
paths
 
and
 
estimate
 
individualized
 
treatment
 
effects.

layers,
 
8
 
attention
 
heads,

dimension
 
256,
 
dropout
 
0.1,

4.  Research  Gap  
Despite  the  recent  progress  in  the  field,  several  important  gaps  remain.  Most  sepsis  AI  
studies
 
still
 
focus
 
on
 
risk
 
prediction
 
rather
 
than
 
treatment
 
effect
 
estimation.
 
Such
 
models
 
identify
 
high-risk
 
patients
 
well,
 
however
 
they
 
do
 
not
 
directly
 
estimate
 
which
 
intervention
 
is
 
most
 
likely
 
to
 
improve
 
the
 
outcome.
 
Furthermore,
 
treatment-aware
 
studies
 
are
 
often
 
narrow
 
in
 
scope.
 
Many
 
focus
 
on
 
one
 
intervention
 
at
 
a
 
time,
 
such
 
as
 
antibiotic
 
timing
 
or
 
fluid

rather
 
than

all

used
 
in
 
sepsis
 
care
 
(as
 
a
 
result
 
there
 
is
 
no
 
actual
 
analysis
 
of
 
which
 
treatment
 
is
 
better
 
for

current
 
case
 
at
 
hand).
 
Bias
 
from
 
patient
 
differences
 
also
 
remains
 
as
 
an
 
absolute
 
challenge.
 
In

ICU
 
data,
 
treatment
 
assignment
 
is
 
not
 
random,
 
so
 
models
 
can
 
easily

Not

that

personalized

5.  Expected  Contributions  
This  study  is  expected  to  make  four  main  contributions  to  the  field.  First,  it  will  develop  a  
causal
 
deep
 
learning
 
framework
 
for
 
individualized
 
sepsis
 
treatment
 
effect
 
prediction,
 
rather
 
than
 
adding
 
to
 
the
 
large
 
number
 
of
 
studies
 
that
 
only
 
detect
 
sepsis
 
onset,
 
with
 
the
 
targeted
 
metrics
 
of:
 
1.  Classification:  >  0.8  AUROC,  0.4~0.7  AUPRC,  F1  >  0.65  2.  Error:  0.1~0.2  MAE,  0.15~0.25  RMSE  3.  Recommendation:  0.1~0.2  PEHE,  0.1~0.2  ATE  error  
Second,  the  model  will  not  treat  the  prediction  as  a  single-intervention  personalization  
and
 
instead
 
build
 
a
 
multi-intervention
 
sepsis
 
decision
 
support
 
system
 
that
 
includes
 
antibiotics,
 
fluids,
 
and
 
vasopressors.
 
Third,
 
it
 
will

patient
 
time-series
 
modeling
 
with
 
causal
 
adjustment
 
in

single
 
framework.
 
Fourth,
 
it
 
will
 
use
 
a
 
stronger
 
evaluation
 
design
 
based
 
on
 
both
 
real
 
patient
 
data
 
and
 
semi-synthetic
 
simulation.
